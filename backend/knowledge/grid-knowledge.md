# GRID Knowledge Base

## What GRID is
GRID (brand name: The GRID Card, website: thegridcard.com) is a verified career-identity platform for Indian and global students. It connects students, companies and colleges in one place. Every student builds a verified career profile (their "GRID Card") that companies can trust, because profiles are gated behind verification.
Tagline: Your Career. Verified. Transparent. Guided.

Three types of users:
- Students / Freshers: build a verified profile, get an AI career roadmap, take skill exams, apply to jobs.
- Companies / Recruiters: post jobs, target specific colleges, hire verified candidates.
- Colleges / TPOs: manage students, run placement drives, generate placement reports.

## For Students
Registering: Sign up at thegridcard.com with email + password, or "Continue with Google" (Google sign-in is available for students only, not companies). Email must be verified before you can publish your profile or apply to jobs.

The GRID Card: Your verified career identity, an ATM-style card showing your profile photo, name, phone number, unique GRID number, a QR code, and a URL that leads directly to your CV, plus the GRID brand name. You can publish it publicly and share it with employers.

Verification: Your email is verified via a link/OTP. Aadhaar and DigiLocker verification are coming soon.

Publishing and applying gates: You cannot publish your GRID profile publicly, or apply to jobs, until your email is verified. This keeps the network trustworthy for recruiters.

GRID moves/updates: You get 3 free profile updates ("moves") per month. It resets to 3 free on the 1st of each month. Paid top-ups are not active yet; no payments are being taken until Razorpay registration is complete.

AI Career Roadmap: Answer a short quiz and GRID's AI suggests matching careers, then generates a step-by-step roadmap for your chosen career (timeline, salary, milestones, skills). You get 3 roadmap regenerations per month. It works for any career.

Skill Exams and Certification: Skill exams are based on the skills you list in your profile page. You must take the exam for a skill for it to show as verified globally on GRID. Verified skills appear on your public GRID profile.

Documents / background verification: You can upload documents (10th, 12th, degree, post-graduate, experience letters, EPFO/EPF passbook, ID proof) from the Documents tab in your profile. Accepted formats are PDF, JPG and PNG, maximum 5MB each. Documents are private and only shared when you apply to a company. The GRID team reviews and marks documents as Verified.

Courses: Courses are coming soon.

Applying to jobs: Browse jobs and apply (email must be verified). Track applications in the Job Tracker. Stages: Applied, Shortlisted, Interview, Hired, Rejected. If a company rejects you, they must give a reason, so you always know where you stand.

Deleting your account: Settings, then Danger Zone, then Delete Account. A deleted account cannot log back in.

## For Companies / Recruiters
Registering: Companies can register themselves at thegridcard.com, or the GRID team can register on their behalf. It depends on what the HR team or company prefers. Companies log in with their company email and password (Google sign-in is not used for companies).

Posting jobs: Post a job with title, description, required skills, job type, location, salary, openings and deadline. You can target a specific partner college or leave it open to all students.

Hiring pipeline: Move applicants through Applied, Shortlisted, Interview, Hired or Rejected. Marking a candidate "Hired" means an offer is released and they count as placed.

Mandatory feedback: Companies must select a reason when rejecting a candidate. The reason is shared with the student. This is a GRID requirement.

Search Talent (Database Access): Search verified student profiles by skill, location and status, and reach out even if they have not applied. Paid database access will work once Razorpay registration is complete.

Company verification: New companies are pending verification. The GRID team verifies companies, usually within about a week.

Deleting the account: Company Dashboard, Settings, Account, Danger Zone, Delete Account.

## For Colleges / TPOs
Getting an account: Colleges do not self-register. The GRID team onboards them and creates the account. Login at thegridcard.com/college-login.

What TPOs can do: See students linked to their college, view GRID-published and skill-verified students, run placement drives (with eligibility rules for branch, CGPA and batch), and generate placement reports (student-wise, branch-wise, drive-wise) as CSV and branded PDF suitable for NAAC/NBA.

Drive Management: Create, edit and delete drives, set eligibility, see who is eligible and who applied.

Reports: Download placement reports as CSV or PDF with college name, date and formatted tables.

Deleting the account: College Portal, Settings, Danger Zone, Delete Account.

## Common questions
Is GRID free? Yes. Everything on GRID is free right now. Paid plans are coming soon.

What makes GRID different from Naukri or LinkedIn? Every profile is verified, so recruiters can trust what they see. Students cannot publish or apply until verified. Companies must give a reason when rejecting a candidate.

I forgot my password. Students and companies: use "Forgot password" on the login page. Colleges: contact the GRID team, since colleges are onboarded by us.

How do I get verified? Verify your email via the link or OTP sent when you sign up. Aadhaar and DigiLocker verification are coming soon.

Contact and support: A dedicated support email address is coming soon. For now, please use the contact options on thegridcard.com.
